Treatment & Management

While there is no single cure for PCOS, there are many ways to manage it through medication, nutrition, exercise, and lifestyle habits.

Medical:

  • Hormonal birth control: Regulates menstrual cycles and reduces androgen-related symptoms.
  • Anti-androgens: spironolactone to reduce hirsutism and acne.
  • Metformin: Improves insulin sensitivity and may promote ovulation.
  • Fertility treatments: Clomiphene, Letrozole, IVF for women trying to conceive.

Lifestyle Modifications:

  • Diet: Low glycemic index (GI), high-fiber, anti-inflammatory foods.
  • Aerobic Exercise: Regular physical activity improves insulin sensitivity and reduces weight.
  • Weight management: Even 5-10% weight loss can significantly improve symptoms.

Alternative Therapies:

    Benefits:

    • Improves the way your body uses insulin, which is often out of balance in PCOS.
    • Supports ovulation and regular periods.
    • May improve fertility and lower testosterone.

  • Healthy fats found in fatty fish like salmon, or in supplements.

    Benefits:

    • Reduces inflammation, which is common in PCOS.
    • Improves cholesterol levels and supports heart health.
    • May reduce insulin resistance and support weight loss efforts.

  • A vitamin your body makes from sunlight; often low in people with PCOS.

    Benefits:

    • May improve insulin sensitivity and lower blood sugar.
    • Supports hormone regulation.
    • Helps with mood and immune function.

  • A natural compound in turmeric, the yellow spice.

    Benefits:

    • Acts as a powerful anti-inflammatory.
    • May improve cholesterol levels and blood sugar.
    • Supports liver function and helps regulate metabolism.

  • A trace mineral found in small amounts in food.

    Benefits:

    • Enhances how your body uses insulin.
    • May improve ovulation and support hormone balance.
    • Some evidence it lowers cholesterol.

  • An antioxidant that your body makes naturally.

    Benefits:

    • Supports energy production in cells.
    • May improve egg quality and fertility.
    • Helps reduce oxidative stress and may improve insulin resistance.

  • A mineral involved in hundreds of body processes.

    Benefits:

    • Helps regulate blood sugar and insulin.
    • Can reduce cramps, mood swings, and improve sleep.
    • Supports heart and nerve function.

  • A nutrient that helps the body turn fat into energy.

    Benefits:

    • May improve weight loss and fat metabolism.
    • Supports ovulation and menstrual regularity.
    • Helps with energy levels and muscle recovery.

  • A mineral that acts as an antioxidant.

    Benefits:

    • May reduce testosterone levels and improve acne.
    • Supports thyroid health (important, as thyroid issues often overlap with PCOS).
    • Can improve egg quality.
