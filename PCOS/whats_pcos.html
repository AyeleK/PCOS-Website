What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a complex hormonal disorder affecting people of reproductive age. It impacts metabolic, reproductive, and psychological health, often presenting with irregular periods, excess androgens, and ovarian cysts. PCOS is estimated to affect 5-20% of women, depending on diagnostic criteria.

Cause of PCOS

Hormonal imbalance:

  • Elevated androgens (male hormones) disrupt the development of ovarian follicles.
  • Increased luteinizing hormone (a hormone that triggers the release of an egg from the ovary and helps the body prepare for a potential pregnancy) and decreased follicle-stimulating hormone (females is a hormone that helps the ovaries develop follicles, which contain the eggs, and also helps the body produce estrogen).
  • Insulin resistance plays a central role (insulin increases androgen production).

Genetic predisposition:

  • Family history of PCOS or type 2 diabetes increases risk.

Environmental & lifestyle factors:

  • Diet, sedentary lifestyle, obesity may exacerbate hormonal imbalances.

Neuroendocrine and inflammatory pathways:

  • Chronic low-grade inflammation contributes to PCOS development.
  • Gut microbiota may also play a role.

Symptoms of PCOS

  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Excess facial and body hair (hirsutism)
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Thinning hair or male-pattern baldness
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Darkening of the skin, especially along neck creases, groin, and underneath breasts
  • Difficulty with fertility (anovulation)
  • Polycystic ovaries on ultrasound
